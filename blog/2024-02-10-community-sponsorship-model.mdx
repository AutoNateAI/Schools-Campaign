## The Traditional Model Is Broken

  <div className="feature-card">
    <span className="feature-card-icon">üìÖ</span>
    <h3>The Budget Bottleneck</h3>
    <p><strong>The problem:</strong> Educational innovation moves at the speed of budget cycles.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Budget planning happens 12-18 months in advance</li>
      <li>Funds are allocated to existing programs</li>
      <li>New initiatives compete with established priorities</li>
      <li>By the time funding arrives, the moment has passed</li>
    </ul>
    <p><em>Innovation requires agility. Budgets require patience.</em></p>
  </div>

  <div className="feature-card">

    <h3>The Equity Gap</h3>
    <p><strong>The problem:</strong> Schools in wealthy areas can fundraise. Schools in under-resourced areas can't.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>PTAs in affluent districts raise $500-$1,000 per student</li>
      <li>PTAs in low-income districts raise $50-$100 per student</li>
      <li>The gap compounds year after year</li>
      <li>Students who need innovation most get it last</li>
    </ul>
    <p><em>Funding models perpetuate inequality.</em></p>
  </div>

  <div className="feature-card">
    <span className="feature-card-icon">üîÑ</span>
    <h3>The Pilot Paradox</h3>
    <p><strong>The problem:</strong> You need data to get funding, but you need funding to get data.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Districts want proof before investing</li>
      <li>But pilots require upfront investment</li>
      <li>Small-scale tests don't generate compelling data</li>
      <li>So nothing moves forward</li>
    </ul>
    <p><em>The system is stuck.</em></p>
