## What "Ethical AI Use" Actually Means

Ethical AI use isn't about following rules‚Äîit's about **critical thinking applied to technology**.

  <div className="feature-card">

    <ul>

      <li><em>"Whose voices are missing?"</em></li>

  <div className="feature-card">

      <li><em>"What's the source?"</em></li>

    <ul>
      <li><strong>Uncritical use:</strong> Copies the numbers into their report</li>

      <li><em>"Should I cite this as AI-assisted?"</em></li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>Example:</strong> A student uses AI to brainstorm essay ideas.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Uncritical use:</strong> Submits AI-generated text as their own</li>

      <li><em>"Am I being shown what I want to see?"</em></li>

### We Teach the "Why" Behind the Rules

Instead of saying *"Don't plagiarize"*, we ask:

- *"What's the difference between using AI to brainstorm vs. using it to write for you?"*
- *"When does AI enhance your thinking? When does it replace it?"*
- *"How do you maintain intellectual ownership while using powerful tools?"*

**Students develop ethical reasoning, not just rule-following.**
